Setup Guide — Chimera Lite

How It Works

Chimera Lite swaps your face in real time on a remote GPU and streams the result back to your PC. The swapped video is served as a Browser Source that OBS captures. From OBS, you route the video through DroidCam's virtual camera — which apps like WhatsApp Desktop, Zoom, Google Meet, and others recognize as a real webcam.

Step 1 — Install DroidCam Drivers

Three installers are bundled with the app. Install them in this order:

  1. DroidCam.Drivers.7.1.2.exe — Installs the DroidCam virtual webcam device that other apps see.
  2. DroidCam.OBSVirtualOut.Plugin.0.2.2.exe — OBS plugin that routes video to the DroidCam virtual device.
  3. DroidCamOBS.Setup.2.4.2.exe — The DroidCam OBS client (optional, for mobile camera integration).

After installing, restart your PC once for the virtual camera driver to register.

Step 2 — Configure OBS Studio

Open OBS Studio (download from obsproject.com if you don't have it).

2a. Add the Browser Source

  1. In the Sources panel, click + → Browser.
  2. Name it Chimera Face Swap (or anything you like).
  3. Set the URL to: http://localhost:7891
  4. Set Width to 512 and Height to 288.
  5. Check "Shutdown source when not visible" to save resources when you switch scenes.
  6. Click OK. You should see a black rectangle — it will show the swapped video once a session is running.

2b. Set Up Audio (Microphone)

  1. Go to Settings → Audio.
  2. Under Mic/Auxiliary Audio, select your real microphone (e.g. Microphone (Realtek Audio)).
  3. Leave Desktop Audio on Default or disabled — you only need your mic for calls.
  4. Click Apply → OK.

2c. Scene Layout

  1. Right-click the Browser Source in the preview → Transform → Fit to Screen. This fills the canvas.
  2. That's your only source — the face-swapped video. Your real mic is captured separately via the audio settings.

Step 3 — Activate DroidCam Virtual Output

This is the key step that makes WhatsApp and other apps see your swapped face as a real webcam.

  1. In OBS, go to the menu bar → Tools → DroidCam OBS Virtual Output.
  2. Click Start in the dialog that appears.
  3. The status should change to "Active". OBS is now routing the canvas output (your swapped face) to the DroidCam virtual camera device.
Why DroidCam instead of OBS Virtual Camera?
WhatsApp Desktop, some Zoom versions, and other apps don't recognize the default OBS Virtual Camera. DroidCam's virtual device registers as a standard DirectShow webcam, so every app sees it — WhatsApp, Zoom, Meet, Teams, Discord, etc.

Step 4 — Start Chimera Lite

  1. In the Chimera Lite app, upload your source face (the identity photo you want to appear as).
  2. Click Start Session. Wait for the GPU pod to boot (~30-90 seconds).
  3. Once connected, your webcam feed is sent to the GPU, swapped in real time, and streamed back.
  4. Check the OBS preview — your Browser Source should now show the face-swapped video.

Step 5 — Make a Video Call

  1. Open WhatsApp Desktop (or Zoom, Meet, Discord, etc.).
  2. Start a video call. When asked for your camera, select "DroidCam Source" from the camera dropdown.
  3. For audio, select your real microphone — this is routed directly, not through OBS.
  4. The other person sees your face-swapped video in real time. Your voice is your real voice.
Tip: Keep OBS running in the background with DroidCam Virtual Output active during the call. If you close OBS, the virtual camera feed stops.

Troubleshooting

  • "DroidCam Source" not showing in WhatsApp — Restart your PC after installing the DroidCam drivers. The virtual camera device needs a reboot to register.
  • Black screen in OBS Browser Source — Make sure a Chimera Lite session is running and the OBS URL matches: http://localhost:7891
  • "Tools → DroidCam OBS Virtual Output" missing — Install DroidCam.OBSVirtualOut.Plugin.0.2.2.exe and restart OBS.
  • Low FPS or laggy video — Use Realtime mode. Ensure your internet upload speed is at least 5 Mbps with <80ms latency to the GPU datacenter.
  • WhatsApp call has no audio — Audio is separate from OBS. In WhatsApp's call settings, select your real microphone and real speakers/headset.
